The Big Bang of Design

Don't let your starting slides look like the early Universe

  • The Big Bang: Hydrogen (~75%), Helium (~25%), and traces of Lithium.
  • The Mystery: Where did the Carbon, Oxygen, and Iron that make up our planet and bodies come from?
  • The Answer: They were cooked in the nuclear furnaces of stars.

A Star

The Proton-Proton Chain

In stars like our Sun, Hydrogen fuses into Helium via the p-p chain:

$$4\text{p} \rightarrow \text{}^{4}\text{He} + 2\text{e}^+ + 2\nu_e + 2\gamma$$

Every second, the Sun converts 600 million tons of Hydrogen into Helium. The lost mass becomes pure energy via $E=mc^2$.

Advanced Burning Stages

Massive stars don't stop at Helium. As they age, gravity compresses the core, raising temperatures to ignite heavier shells.

The Stellar "Onion Skin"

Concentric shells fuse progressively heavier elements:

  • Hydrogen $\rightarrow$ Helium
  • Helium $\rightarrow$ Carbon
  • Carbon $\rightarrow$ Neon
  • Neon $\rightarrow$ Oxygen
  • Oxygen $\rightarrow$ Silicon
  • Silicon $\rightarrow$ Iron

The Iron Dead End

Iron ($\text{}^{56}\text{Fe}$) has the highest binding energy per nucleon of any element in this chain.

  • Fusing elements lighter than Iron releases energy.
  • Fusing Iron requires energy.

Without energy production, the core collapses in a fraction of a second, triggering a Type II Supernova.

Forging Elements Beyond Iron

How do we get Gold, Platinum, and Uranium? Neutron Capture Processes.

  1. s-process (Slow): Occurs in giant stars; neutrons are captured slowly over decades.
  2. r-process (Rapid): Occurs in extreme environments like Neutron Star Mergers; nuclei are flooded with neutrons in milliseconds.

Summary

Every heavy atom in your body was once cooked inside a star or forged in a violent cosmic collision.
